Overview

Topic Explanations
Given your topic framework, research each topic and subtopic and write a paragraph for each.
What to include in your paragraph:
1.  Definitions
2.  Importance/ How it relates
3.  Basic Explanation of the concept/topic/idea
Exercise and Lab Ideas
Think up 1 lab and 1 exercise that you could do to showcase your topic.
An Exercise is a non-computer way of explaining an idea. A lab is the computer or technical/digital way
to showcase a topic or subtopic. Labs don’t need to be created by you. For example, to teach
programming you could suggest using Code Academy, but you’d need to be sure to include discussion
points or milestones throughout the lab.
Example Exercise:
Files vs Folders and Storage
Students get a piece of paper and write on it. When they’re ready to “save” it, they call it something and
place it into a manila folder. They name the folder and place it into a box which represents the hard
drive. Instructor wil  also place a paper in it to represent a file. This is to showcase that a Hard drive
holds folders and files, folders can hold files, but files can’t hold folders. Can also showcase moving files
by taking a students paper out of one folder and moving it to another. This shows that although the
folder is empty, it stil  exists.
Example Lab:
Students wil  create a folder on their computer, create a file, move it around and delete it.
